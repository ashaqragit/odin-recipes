Kabsa

Description

Kabsa is an amazingly flavored rice dish that can be made with chicken, beef or lamb. An aromatic rice pilaf, a one pot meal from the Arabian peninsula. It is like having a flavorful rice and chicken kebab, all made in one pot. An exotic Saudi rice dish served with raisins and almonds in holidays and any festive season.

Ingredients

  1. Chicken
  2. Rice
  3. Onion
  4. Garlic
  5. Water
  6. Ghee
  7. Tomato paste
  8. Salt
  9. Spices:
    1. Paprika
    2. Coriander
    3. Black Pepper
    4. Cardamom
    5. cloves
    6. Tumeric
    7. Cinnamon
    8. Dry Lime
    9. Bay Leaves

Steps

  1. In a non stick deep skillet or a dutch oven, heat oil / ghee or butter and toast spices until fragrant.
  2. Add garlic and onion. Saute until translucent.
  3. Add chicken thighs and cook for about 2 minutes on each side until no longer pink from outside.
  4. Add rice, tomato paste and check seasonings.
  5. Mix everything well together.
  6. Add water or chicken broth if using and cook rice until done.

Instant Pot Instructions:

  1. Turn on the IP on the saute option melt ghee then add the spices and toast for a couple of minutes.
  2. Add garlic and onions and continue to saute until translucent or for about 3 minutes more.
  3. Add chicken and cook for about 5 minutes on both sides or until no longer pink.
  4. Mix in rice, tomato paste and salt. Stir well, bringing chicken pieces to top.
  5. Close the lid and turn it to pressure cook for 8 minutes. When it is done let it natural release for 5 minutes then release the rest manually. Now it might give you the burn warning. I've used less water, 1.5 cups of water for 1 cup of rice. This water ratio is high as most people recommend 1:1. My IP did not give me the burn warning, rice was a little bit mushy but no one complained. I've had a follower message me that when it gave them the burn warning, they turned their pot off, scraped the bottom then turned it on again and it worked just fine.
